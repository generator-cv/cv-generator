Career Tips

Expert CV Writing Tips

Proven advice to help you write a CV that stands out and lands interviews in 2026.

💡
Loading your first tip…
Tip 1 of 10
🎯

Tailor Every Application

Customize your CV for each role you apply to. Match keywords from the job description to pass ATS filters and immediately grab the recruiter's attention.

📊

Quantify Your Achievements

Use numbers wherever possible — "Increased team efficiency by 40%" beats "improved team performance" every time. Metrics build instant credibility.

✍️

Keep It Concise — 1 or 2 Pages

Recruiters scan CVs in under 10 seconds. One to two pages is the gold standard. Cut anything that doesn't directly support the role you want.

🔍

Proofread Thoroughly

A single spelling mistake can cost you an interview. Read your CV backwards to catch errors, then ask a trusted friend or colleague to review it too.

📧

Use a Professional Email

Your contact email should be firstname.lastname@gmail.com or similar. Outdated or quirky handles from your school days signal unprofessionalism.

💼

Lead With Strong Action Verbs

Start every bullet point with a dynamic verb: "Led", "Designed", "Launched", "Achieved", "Reduced". This makes your contributions sound confident and decisive.
